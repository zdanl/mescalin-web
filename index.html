Bringing privacy back.

We are working on novel technologies to let you take privacy back into your hands. Meanwhile, we do Python and Javascript coding, and conduct security audits.

Get Started

Built with Python 3.11 and LLVM 15.0.3

We are aiming for monolithic compilation of Python3 code to Verilog in a form of LLVM IR optimized, automated high-to-low level source code synthesis. This may be used for FPGA Accelerators but also for the design and implementation of manufactured CPUs. Here are several visions for the future of semiconductor manufacturing, in general security and privacy of modern compute, and variations of hacker spirit. We feel like a development toolchain to accomodate the future is of paramount importance, just as much as features like Instruction Set Randomization, integrity of CPU and network chip architecture and encryption. Then profit.

Energy-efficiency and Instruction Set Randomization

Our quantum cobalt chips are meant to be low on picojoules per flop and provide maximum quality on fast encryption, stable entanglement, SRAM storage of secrets and protection against hostile interference. We will provide ultra random numbers using photonic noise.

Hardware Accelerated Crypto

Neither our CPUs nor network cores will carry dynamic microcode firmware, but instead display fully static electrical gate arrays past synthesized hardware description modeling. These are highly optimized by LLVM, and are to implement in physical electric semiconductivity symmetric and asymmetric crypto/encodings

Projects

The goals and mission of privacy hardware and software are endless, but our main interests currently involve learning and optimizing the current of traditional and quantum conductivity, overcoming cryptographic & computational complexity, buildchains and multi-cross-platform overhead & obstacles of high-level programming languages operating on bits & bytes.
